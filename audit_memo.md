# Audit Memo: What Public Benchmarks Miss About Tenacious

Public tool-use benchmarks miss the failure surface that actually determines whether Tenacious outreach earns trust or burns it. They can tell us whether an agent completed a sequence, but not whether it softened a weak public signal, stayed inside real bench capacity, or moved a warm lead into a discovery call without a timezone or handoff error.

**Gap 1: public-signal restraint is not the same as factual correctness.** Week 10 probes `HS-001`, `HS-002`, `REL-001`, and `REL-003` show the core failure is over-claiming from weak or stale evidence, not failing to mention a signal at all. In traces `tr_d836ebb13a91` and `tr_fe2b1396e43c`, the workflow can compose and send a grounded outreach step, but a generic benchmark would still over-credit the output even if it turned a single open-role hint into a confident “aggressive hiring” claim. Tenacious-Bench therefore needs a dimension for hiring-signal over-claiming and a separate dimension for public-signal reliability.

**Gap 2: bench honesty is a sales-quality constraint, not just an operations concern.** Probes `BOC-001`, `BOC-002`, `BOC-003`, and `BOC-004` show that a persuasive message can still be unacceptable if it promises a team shape or start date the bench cannot support. Traces `tr_4c1c7ea77e33` and `tr_01246c6c745b` show the Week 10 flow can qualify a reply and advance toward booking, but tau2-style benchmarks do not inspect whether the offer matched `bench_summary.json` before the meeting was proposed. This is a distinct benchmark gap because the harm is commercial: a booked call based on false capacity is worse than no call.

**Gap 3: Tenacious tone failures are not generic style nits.** Probes `TONE-001`, `TONE-002`, and `TONE-004` show that vendor-sounding language like “A-player bench,” “offshore talent,” or empty follow-up phrasing damages positioning even when the workflow otherwise completes. Trace `tr_d836ebb13a91` matters here because it is exactly where the system can be fluent while still sounding like a commodity staffing shop. Public benchmarks usually grade helpfulness or politeness, but they do not grade whether the copy violates a company-specific operating voice.

**Gap 4: scheduling quality must be graded as part of conversion, not after it.** Probes `TZ-001`, `TZ-003`, and `TZ-004` show that once a lead is warm, timezone math and overlap discipline become part of the sales outcome. Traces `tr_01246c6c745b` and `tr_798431a02fa8` show the Week 10 system can reach Cal.com booking and CRM sync, but public benchmarks do not ask whether the proposed slots were overlap-safe for East Africa, the EU, and the US. A wrong meeting handoff is a failed conversion, not a minor formatting issue.

**Gap 5: competitor-gap quality depends on evidence humility, not stronger rhetoric.** Probes `GAP-001`, `GAP-003`, and `GAP-004` show the hard case is not “mention competitors,” but “extract a narrow research finding without sounding condescending or inventing peer behavior.” Trace `tr_fe2b1396e43c` is relevant because an email can look strong while still overstating what peer evidence proves. This is the non-obvious benchmark gap: the agent must convert sparse public evidence into a bounded research claim rather than a sharper pitch.

Tenacious-Bench v0.1 is built around these mutually distinct gaps: hiring-signal over-claiming, public-signal reliability, bench over-commitment, tone/style drift, scheduling handoff correctness, competitor-gap over-claiming, and ICP misclassification. The point is not to replace general benchmarks. It is to grade the failure surface that Week 10 evidence showed public benchmarks cannot see.
